# Guidelines on Digital Lending
**Circular Number:** RBI/2022-23/111  
**Date:** September 02, 2022  
**Issued by:** Reserve Bank of India  
**Reference:** DOR.CRE.REC.66/21.07.001/2022-23

## Applicability
These guidelines apply to:
- All Commercial Banks (excluding Payments Banks, State Co-operative Banks, and District Central Co-operative Banks)
- All Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks
- All Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs, including Housing Finance Companies)

Collectively referred to as **Regulated Entities (REs)**.

---

## 1. Background
1.1 The rapid growth of Digital Lending has necessitated a comprehensive regulatory framework to protect borrowers and ensure orderly growth of the digital lending ecosystem in India.

1.2 These guidelines are based on the recommendations of the Working Group on Digital Lending including Lending through Online Platforms and Mobile Apps (WGDL).

---

## 2. Key Definitions

2.1 **"Lending Service Provider (LSP)"** means an agent of a RE who carries out one or more of lender's functions in customer acquisition, underwriting support, pricing support, disbursement, servicing, monitoring, collection, liquidation of specific loan or loan portfolio on behalf of REs in conformity with applicable laws and regulations.

2.2 **"Digital Lending App (DLA)"** means mobile and web-based applications with a user interface that facilitate digital lending services by REs or LSPs.

2.3 **"Key Fact Statement (KFS)"** means a statement disclosing key facts of a loan agreement in simplified and standardised format for the borrower.

2.4 **"Annual Percentage Rate (APR)"** means the annual rate charged for borrowing or earned through an investment, expressed as a percentage that represents the actual yearly cost of funds over the term of a loan.

---

## 3. Disbursement and Repayment

3.1 **Obligation:** All loan disbursements and repayments shall be executed only between the bank accounts of the borrower and the Regulated Entity, without any pass-through or pool account of the LSP or any third party.

3.2 Any fees, charges, etc., payable to LSPs shall be paid directly by the RE and shall not be charged to the borrower.

3.3 **Prohibition:** Collection of any fees directly from the borrower by the LSP or any third party is strictly prohibited.

3.4 **Prohibition:** REs shall not permit LSPs to access borrowers' mobile phone resources like files, media, contact list, call logs, telephony functions, etc.

---

## 4. Transparency – Key Fact Statement (KFS)

4.1 REs shall provide a KFS to the borrower before the execution of the loan contract.

4.2 The KFS shall mandatorily include:
- Annual Percentage Rate (APR)
- Recovery mechanism and grievance redressal mechanism
- Details of LSP involved, if any
- Cooling-off / look-up period

4.3 **Deadline:** The KFS must be provided at least **24 hours** before the loan disbursement to allow the borrower to review.

---

## 5. Cooling-Off Period

5.1 Borrowers shall be given an explicit cooling-off period, during which they can exit the digital loan by paying back the principal and the proportionate APR without any pre-payment penalty.

5.2 For loans with tenure of 7 days or less, cooling-off period shall be **1 day**.

5.3 For loans with tenure of more than 7 days, cooling-off period shall be **3 days**.

---

## 6. Data Privacy and Storage

6.1 **Obligation:** Data collected by DLAs shall be need-based, with prior and explicit consent of the borrower.

6.2 **Prohibition:** DLAs shall not store data on servers located outside of India.

6.3 Personal information of the borrower shall be collected through the DLA only after obtaining clear, informed consent.

6.4 Borrowers shall have the option to deny consent or revoke previously granted consent.

---

## 7. Grievance Redressal

7.1 Each RE shall have a nodal grievance redressal officer to deal with complaints relating to digital lending.

7.2 **Obligation:** All loan servicing and repayment-related information shall be reported to Credit Information Companies (CICs) by REs.

7.3 **Deadline:** Complaints must be resolved within **30 days** of receipt.

7.4 If the complaint is not resolved within **30 days**, the borrower may escalate to the RBI Ombudsman.

---

## 8. Penalties for Non-Compliance

8.1 Non-compliance with these guidelines shall result in:
- Monetary penalty up to ₹50,00,000 (Fifty Lakh Rupees)
- Cancellation of LSP partnership authorisation
- Prohibition from onboarding new digital lending customers for up to **6 months**

8.2 Wilful data privacy violations attract additional penalties under ₹25,00,000 (Twenty-Five Lakh Rupees).
