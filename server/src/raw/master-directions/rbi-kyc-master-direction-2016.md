# Master Direction – Know Your Customer (KYC) Direction, 2016
**Circular Number:** RBI/DBR/2015-16/18  
**Date:** February 25, 2016  
**Updated:** March 01, 2023  
**Issued by:** Department of Banking Regulation, Reserve Bank of India  

## Applicability
These Directions are applicable to:
- All Scheduled Commercial Banks (including Small Finance Banks and Payments Banks)
- All Primary (Urban) Co-operative Banks / State Co-operative Banks / Central Co-operative Banks
- All Non-Banking Financial Companies (including Housing Finance Companies)
- All Payment System Providers and Payment System Participants

---

## Part A – General

### 1. Short Title and Commencement
1.1 These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Know Your Customer) Directions, 2016.
1.2 These Directions shall come into force with immediate effect.

### 2. Definitions
2.1 **"Beneficial Owner"** means the natural person(s), who ultimately owns or controls a client and/or the person on whose behalf a transaction is being conducted and includes a person who exercises ultimate effective control over a juridical person or arrangement.

2.2 **"Customer Due Diligence (CDD)"** means identifying and verifying the customer and the beneficial owner using reliable, independent source documents, data or information.

2.3 **"Politically Exposed Person (PEP)"** means individuals who are or have been entrusted with prominent public functions in a foreign country, such as Heads of States or Governments, senior politicians, senior government or judicial or military officers, senior executives of state-owned corporations, important political party officials, etc.

2.4 **"Non-Face-to-Face Customer"** means a customer who is not physically present at the time of account opening.

2.5 **"Video based Customer Identification Process (V-CIP)"** means an alternate method of customer identification with the aid of Video call between the bank official and the prospective customer.

---

## Part B – Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Procedure

### 3. KYC Policy
3.1 Every Regulated Entity (RE) shall frame its KYC Policy duly approved by its Board of Directors which shall incorporate the following:
- Customer Acceptance Policy
- Risk Management Procedures
- Customer Identification Procedures (CIP)
- Monitoring of Transactions

### 4. Customer Acceptance Policy
4.1 REs shall not open an account or carry out any transaction if they are unable to apply appropriate CDD measures.

4.2 **Prohibition:** REs shall not open anonymous accounts or accounts in fictitious / benami names.

4.3 REs shall identify the beneficial owner(s) for all accounts opened for legal entities and update KYC information at periodic intervals.

### 5. Customer Identification Procedure (CIP)
5.1 REs shall verify the identity and address of the customers before commencing any account-based relationship.

5.2 Individuals shall be identified using any of the following Officially Valid Documents (OVDs):
- Passport
- Driving License
- Voter Identity Card
- Proof of possession of Aadhaar number
- NREGA Job Card
- Letter issued by the Government of India

5.3 REs shall submit Aadhaar e-KYC authentication requests only after obtaining the consent of the customer.

---

## Part C – Periodic Updation and Risk Categorisation

### 6. Periodic Updation of KYC
6.1 REs shall carry out periodic updation of KYC at the following intervals:
- High Risk customers: At least once in **2 years**
- Medium Risk customers: At least once in **8 years**
- Low Risk customers: At least once in **10 years**

6.2 REs shall ensure that existing accounts are made fully KYC compliant within a period of **6 months** from the date of these Directions becoming effective.

6.3 Failure to complete KYC updation within the prescribed period may result in restriction of operations of the account.

### 7. Customer Risk Categorisation
7.1 REs shall carry out risk assessment exercise to arrive at a risk categorisation of customers.

7.2 All customers shall be categorised as:
- Low Risk
- Medium Risk
- High Risk

7.3 Customers who are Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs), Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), or dealing in high-value cash transactions shall be categorised as **High Risk**.

---

## Part D – Monitoring of Transactions

### 8. Transaction Monitoring
8.1 REs shall have in place a robust software to throw alerts when transactions are inconsistent with the customer's risk profile.

8.2 REs shall preserve all records of transactions for a minimum period of **5 years** from the date of the transaction.

8.3 **Cash transactions** exceeding ₹10,00,000 (Ten Lakh Rupees) in a month shall be reported to the Financial Intelligence Unit – India (FIU-IND).

8.4 REs shall file a Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) with FIU-IND within **7 working days** of being satisfied that the transaction is suspicious.

---

## Part E – Penalties and Enforcement

### 9. Non-compliance Penalties
9.1 Non-compliance with these Directions shall attract penalty as prescribed under:
- Section 47A(1) of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949
- Section 58G of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934

9.2 Penalties may include:
- Monetary penalty of up to ₹1,00,00,000 (One Crore Rupees) for serious violations
- Penalty of ₹10,000 per day for continuing violations
- Cancellation of licence in cases of wilful non-compliance

---

## Part F – Video-based Customer Identification Process (V-CIP)

### 10. V-CIP Guidelines
10.1 REs opting to use V-CIP shall obtain prior approval from RBI and comply with the following conditions:

10.2 The video interaction shall be live and not pre-recorded.

10.3 The RE shall capture a clear photograph of the customer during the V-CIP interaction.

10.4 The RE shall obtain the Aadhaar number during the video call and perform OTP-based authentication.

10.5 REs shall maintain audit trail of the V-CIP process including time-stamp and geo-tag for at least **8 years**.
